Don't Starve Together challenges new players with its unforgiving survival mechanics, but mastering the first 20 days sets the foundation for long-term success. Your primary objectives are maintaining hunger above 50%, avoiding complete darkness, and establishing a sustainable base before winter arrives on day 21. The game operates on a 16-minute day cycle with 8 minutes of daylight, 4 minutes of dusk, and 4 minutes of night, requiring careful time management for resource gathering and exploration.

## Character Selection and Starting Strategy

Wilson remains the optimal choice for beginners due to his beard growth mechanic providing 45 insulation during winter and no stat penalties. His beard can be shaved for beard hair, a crucial crafting material for meat effigies and luxury items. Alternative beginner-friendly characters include Wickerbottom for her crafting recipes knowledge and Wigfrid for combat advantages, though both require specific playstyle adaptations.

Your first day should focus on gathering essential materials while exploring the map. Collect 12 grass tufts, 12 twigs, 20 flint, and 10 logs minimum. These materials craft your basic tools: axe, pickaxe, and shovel. Avoid fighting creatures initially except spiders for silk and monster meat, which can be converted to eggs through a bird in a birdcage.

## Base Location and Early Infrastructure

Selecting the right base location determines your survival success rate. Optimal locations feature proximity to beefalo herds (within 2-3 screens), rabbit holes for consistent food, and multiple biome access including grasslands, forests, and rocky areas. Avoid swamps and desert edges due to hostile creatures and limited resources.

Your initial base requires these structures in order of priority:

1. Fire pit (12 stones, 2 logs) - permanent light source
2. Chest storage (8 logs) - resource organization  
3. Drying racks (3 twigs, 2 rope, 2 charcoal) - food preservation
4. Science machine (4 logs, 4 stones, 1 gold) - advanced crafting

## Resource Management and Food Systems

Food management follows a hierarchy based on hunger restoration and spoilage rates. Raw vegetables spoil in 10 days, cooked vegetables in 15 days, while jerky lasts 20 days. The crock pot becomes essential for efficient food usage, combining ingredients for meals providing higher hunger, health, and sanity restoration.

| Food Type | Hunger Value | Spoilage Time | Best Use |
|-----------|--------------|---------------|----------|
| Berries (raw) | 9.375 | 6 days | Emergency food |
| Cooked berries | 12.5 | 15 days | Crock pot filler |
| Meatballs | 62.5 | 10 days | Primary meal |
| Jerky | 25 | 20 days | Winter storage |

Prioritize meatballs as your staple food, requiring 1 meat (including monster meat) and 3 fillers like berries or vegetables. This recipe provides 62.5 hunger and 5 health, making it highly efficient for resource investment.

## Seasonal Preparation and Winter Survival

Winter preparation begins on day 15 with resource stockpiling and infrastructure expansion. Temperature drops to freezing levels, requiring insulation items and increased fuel consumption. Crops stop growing, forcing reliance on preserved foods and alternative protein sources.

Essential winter preparations include:

- 40+ cooked foods or crock pot meals
- Winter hat (4 silk, 4 beefalo wool) providing 60 insulation
- Thermal stone for portable warmth
- 2+ stacks of logs for fuel
- Rabbit traps near rabbit holes (6+ traps recommended)

Beefalo wool collection requires shaving beefalo during their mating season (days 16-21) when they become aggressive. Use a luxury fan or electric dart for safe shaving, yielding 3 beefalo wool per animal.

## Combat Fundamentals and Threat Management

Combat in Don't Starve Together relies on kiting patterns and timing rather than button mashing. Most creatures have predictable attack patterns allowing hit-and-run tactics. Spiders attack every 1.5 seconds, allowing 2-3 hits between their attacks when properly timed.

Hound attacks occur every 9-13 days starting around day 9, with pack sizes increasing over time. Early hound waves contain 2-4 hounds, escalating to 6-8 hounds by day 50. Defensive strategies include:

- Leading hounds to beefalo herds for natural protection
- Tooth trap placement around base perimeter (6+ traps)
- Tentacle spike crafting for 51 damage per hit
- Panic room construction with walls and healing items

## Advanced Base Development

Once winter survival is secured, expand your base with specialized structures improving efficiency and safety. Bee boxes provide renewable honey when placed near flowers, requiring 4 boards, 2 honeycomb, and 1 bee. Lightning rods protect structures during spring storms, essential for preventing fire damage to your base.

Farms offer renewable food but require significant investment in fertilizer and seeds. Improved farms using rot or manure provide better crop yields than basic farms. However, foraging and hunting typically provide more efficient food sources until late-game scenarios.

## Sanity Management and Mental Health

Sanity decreases from darkness, monster proximity, and eating raw monster meat, while increasing from sleep, cooked green mushrooms, and certain clothing items. Low sanity spawns shadow creatures that deal real damage, making sanity management crucial for survival.

Maintain sanity above 50% through these methods:

- Prototype new items at science machines (+15 sanity each)
- Wear garlands made from 12 petals (+2 sanity per minute)
- Sleep in tents or bedrolls when sanity drops below 50%
- Pick flowers for small sanity boosts (+5 sanity each)

## Multiplayer Coordination Strategies

Don't Starve Together's multiplayer mechanics allow task specialization and resource sharing. Coordinate roles among team members: one player focuses on food production, another handles base construction, while others explore and gather resources. Shared inventories through chests prevent resource hoarding and improve group efficiency.

Resurrection mechanics require life-giving amulets, meat effigies, or touch stones for player revival. Plan resurrection infrastructure early, as player death results in maximum health reduction until revival. Ghost players can haunt objects and provide limited assistance but cannot interact with most items.

Mastering these fundamental systems provides the foundation for surviving your first winter and progressing toward advanced gameplay mechanics. Focus on consistent food production, adequate shelter, and gradual base expansion while learning enemy patterns and seasonal challenges through experience.
