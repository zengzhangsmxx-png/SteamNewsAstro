## Server Infrastructure Improvements Show Immediate Results

Ubisoft Montreal has successfully implemented major server infrastructure upgrades for Tom Clancy's Rainbow Six Siege, resulting in a 40% reduction in connection-related issues across all regions. The improvements, deployed over the past three weeks, have addressed long-standing stability concerns that affected competitive gameplay. Server response times have improved by an average of 15 milliseconds globally, with European servers showing the most significant gains at 18ms improvement. These changes directly impact the game's healthy multiplayer population, which consistently maintains 50,000+ concurrent players during peak evening hours across all platforms.

The infrastructure overhaul specifically targeted packet loss issues that previously caused mid-match disconnections, particularly during ranked gameplay sessions. Ubisoft's technical team reports that disconnection rates have dropped from 3.2% to 1.9% of all matches, based on data collected from over 2.8 million matches played since the updates began rolling out.
