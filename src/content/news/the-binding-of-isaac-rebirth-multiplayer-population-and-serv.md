## Technical Infrastructure and Stability

The Binding of Isaac: Rebirth operates without traditional multiplayer servers, instead utilizing Steam's networking infrastructure for remote play capabilities. This architecture provides several advantages for long-term stability and accessibility.

Local co-op processing occurs entirely on the host machine, with input data from additional controllers handled through direct hardware connections or Steam's controller API. Frame synchronization between players depends on the host system's performance capabilities, with recommended specifications including a consistent 60 FPS output for optimal multiplayer experience.

Steam Remote Play Together functionality enables online co-op through video streaming technology, allowing up to 4 remote players to join local sessions. Connection quality depends on upload bandwidth from the host, with minimum requirements of 10 Mbps for stable 2-player sessions and 20 Mbps for 4-player configurations.

Third-party solutions like Parsec and Moonlight provide alternative remote play options, often delivering superior performance for users with high-speed internet connections. These platforms bypass Steam's compression algorithms, resulting in reduced input latency and improved visual quality during extended co-op sessions.
