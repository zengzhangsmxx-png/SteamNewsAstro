## Windrose Expands Into Underwater Territory

Kraken Express has revealed extensive details about Windrose's first major expansion, Crimson Depths, which introduces underwater exploration mechanics and significantly expands the game's world. The DLC adds six new underwater zones, a complete trident weapon class with 24 unique variants, and 12 cooperative dungeons designed for up to four players. According to the developer's official announcement on April 28, 2026, the expansion will deliver 15-20 hours of additional content when it launches in Q3 2026.

The base game currently maintains a "Very Positive" rating with 87% positive reviews from over 45,000 Steam users, with daily concurrent players averaging between 12,000-18,000 according to SteamDB tracking data. This expansion represents Kraken Express's largest content update since Windrose's early access launch in March 2025.

## New Underwater Exploration Mechanics

The Crimson Depths expansion fundamentally changes how players navigate Windrose's world by introducing full underwater exploration. Players gain access to the Depth Walker ability after completing Chapter 4 of the main campaign, unlocking six distinct underwater biomes including the Coral Labyrinth, Abyssal Trenches, and the titular Crimson Depths.

Each underwater zone features unique environmental challenges that require specific equipment and strategies. The Pressure Gauge system monitors diving depth, with players needing to craft specialized gear to reach deeper areas. Oxygen management becomes crucial during extended exploration sessions, with breathing apparatus upgrades available through the expanded crafting system.

The trident weapon class introduces 24 unique variants, each with distinct movesets and elemental properties. Unlike surface weapons, tridents maintain full effectiveness underwater while suffering reduced damage on land, creating strategic depth for equipment management. The weapon class features a three-hit combo system with charged attacks that consume stamina but deliver area-of-effect damage.

## Cooperative Dungeon System

Crimson Depths introduces 12 cooperative dungeons specifically designed for multiplayer experiences, though all content remains accessible to solo players through AI companion support. Each dungeon scales difficulty based on party size, with enemy health and damage increasing by 25% per additional player beyond the first.

The dungeon system incorporates puzzle mechanics that require coordination between players, such as simultaneous switch activation and timed swimming sequences. Boss encounters feature multi-phase fights where players must work together to exploit enemy weaknesses, with some bosses requiring specific trident types to defeat effectively.

Loot distribution uses a smart system that ensures each player receives appropriate rewards for their character build, with rare materials having guaranteed drop rates for the entire party. Dungeon completion times range from 20-45 minutes depending on difficulty level and party coordination.

## Enhanced Crafting and Progression

The expansion doubles the available crafting materials from 48 to 96 unique items, with underwater-specific resources like Pressure Pearls and Abyssal Crystals. These materials enable crafting of specialized diving equipment, underwater vehicles, and the complete trident weapon line.

A new Aquatic Mastery skill tree adds 30 additional progression nodes focused on underwater combat, exploration efficiency, and breath-holding capabilities. Players can specialize in different underwater roles, from deep-sea explorer to combat diver, with each path offering unique abilities and equipment bonuses.

The housing system receives significant updates with underwater base construction options. Players can build submarine docks, underwater gardens, and pressure-sealed workshops using DLC materials. Base functionality extends to include oxygen generation systems and teleportation networks between surface and underwater locations.

## Technical Performance and Accessibility

Kraken Express has implemented several technical improvements specifically for underwater rendering. The new water physics system supports realistic light refraction and particle effects while maintaining the base game's 60 FPS target on recommended hardware specifications (GTX 1660 Ti, 16GB RAM, Intel i5-10400F).

Accessibility features include colorblind-friendly depth indicators, subtitle support for underwater audio cues, and customizable swimming controls for players with mobility limitations. The pressure gauge system includes both visual and audio warnings to prevent disorientation during deep-sea exploration.

Beta testing begins in July 2026 for Season Pass holders, with open beta access planned for August 2026. The full release targets September 2026, with day-one availability on Steam and included in Xbox Game Pass.

## Pricing and Availability

The Crimson Depths expansion will be available as a standalone purchase for $19.99 USD or included with the Windrose Season Pass ($34.99 USD). Season Pass holders receive additional benefits including exclusive cosmetic items, early beta access, and guaranteed access to two additional DLC releases planned for 2027.

Pre-orders open on June 15, 2026, with a 10% discount for the first week. The expansion requires the base Windrose game and approximately 8GB of additional storage space for the full installation.

This expansion positions Windrose as a comprehensive action-RPG experience that successfully bridges single-player adventure with meaningful cooperative gameplay, addressing community requests for more diverse environments and weapon variety while maintaining the core gameplay loop that has attracted its dedicated player base.
