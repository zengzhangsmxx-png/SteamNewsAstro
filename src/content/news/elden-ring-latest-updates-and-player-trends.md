## Technical Improvements and Visual Enhancements

Patch 1.12 implements three distinct ray tracing modes: reflections, shadows, and global illumination. Each can be independently configured, allowing players to balance visual quality with performance based on their hardware capabilities. The reflections system particularly enhances water surfaces and metallic armor, while ray-traced shadows provide more accurate lighting in dungeon environments.

Performance optimizations extend beyond ray tracing implementation. FromSoftware's patch notes detail improvements to memory allocation that reduce stuttering by approximately 40% in open-world traversal. Frame rate stability has increased across all hardware tiers, with mid-range systems (RTX 3060/RX 6600 XT) seeing 15-20% performance gains in demanding areas like Caelid and Leyndell.

## Hardware Requirements and Compatibility

The ray tracing implementation requires DirectX 12 Ultimate support, limiting compatibility to RTX 20-series, RTX 30-series, RTX 40-series, and AMD RX 6000/7000-series graphics cards. FromSoftware recommends 16GB system RAM for optimal ray tracing performance, though the feature remains functional with 12GB configurations at reduced settings.

VRAM usage has increased modestly with ray tracing enabled. At 1440p resolution with full ray tracing, the game utilizes approximately 9.2GB of video memory—a 1.8GB increase from the previous version. This places additional demands on 8GB graphics cards, though dynamic resolution scaling helps maintain playability.
