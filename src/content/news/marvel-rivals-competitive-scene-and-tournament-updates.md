## Marvel Rivals Championship Series Launches with Major Prize Pool

NetEase Games officially announced the Marvel Rivals Championship Series during their developer livestream on April 22, 2026, revealing a comprehensive competitive ecosystem for their superhero team shooter. The inaugural tournament series will feature a $500,000 total prize pool distributed across regional qualifiers and a global championship finale scheduled for October 2026 in Los Angeles. This announcement comes as Marvel Rivals maintains steady player engagement with approximately 45,000 concurrent players during peak hours according to Steam statistics, positioning the game for serious esports investment.

The championship structure includes four major regions: North America, Europe, Asia-Pacific, and Latin America, with each region receiving dedicated qualifier tournaments starting in May 2026. Regional winners will advance to the global finals, competing for the largest portion of the prize distribution. NetEase partnered with ESL Gaming to organize and broadcast the tournament series, leveraging their established infrastructure for competitive gaming events.

## New Competitive Features and Draft System

Alongside the tournament announcement, NetEase revealed significant updates to Marvel Rivals' competitive infrastructure. The new Competitive Draft mode introduces strategic hero selection phases, allowing teams to ban specific characters before picking their roster. This system addresses community feedback about character balance in high-level play, particularly concerning overpowered combinations like Iron Man and Doctor Strange synergies.

The draft system operates on a snake format: Team A bans one hero, Team B bans one hero, then alternating picks until each team selects six heroes. According to game director Zhang Wei, internal testing showed the draft system reduced match duration variance by 23% while increasing strategic depth. The competitive mode also implements skill-based matchmaking with visible rank progression from Bronze to Grandmaster tiers.

## Tournament Format and Regional Structure

The Marvel Rivals Championship Series follows a multi-stage format designed to accommodate both amateur and professional teams. Regional qualifiers begin with open brackets accepting unlimited team registrations, followed by closed qualifiers for the top 32 teams from each region. The qualification process spans three months, culminating in regional championships that determine global finale participants.

Each regional championship awards $75,000 in prize money, with the global finale distributing the remaining $200,000 among the top eight teams worldwide. Tournament matches use the new Competitive Draft mode exclusively, with additional spectator features including player perspective switching and real-time ability cooldown tracking. ESL will broadcast all matches on Twitch and YouTube, with regional language commentary for major markets.

The tournament ruleset prohibits certain hero combinations identified as problematic during beta testing, specifically the Hulk-Scarlet Witch pairing that demonstrated win rates exceeding 78% in internal competitive matches. Teams must also maintain roster stability, with substitutions limited to emergency situations and requiring tournament official approval.
