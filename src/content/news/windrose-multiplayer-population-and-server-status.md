The connectivity problems manifest as login timeouts averaging 45-60 seconds, matchmaking delays extending up to 3 minutes, and session disconnections occurring every 15-20 minutes during peak gameplay periods. Developer Kraken Express has confirmed these issues stem from database optimization conflicts introduced in the April 21 backend update.

Steam's concurrent player tracking shows Windrose maintaining 8,000-12,000 simultaneous users during European evening hours, representing a 25-30% decrease from the typical 15,000-18,000 range observed prior to the server issues. The game's overall Steam review score remains at 78% positive from 4,847 total reviews, though recent reviews show a temporary dip to 65% positive as players express frustration with connectivity problems.

## Developer Response and Hotfix Timeline

Kraken Express acknowledged the server issues through their official Steam announcement on April 23, citing "unexpected database load patterns following our latest content update" as the primary cause. The development team has deployed three hotfixes within 36 hours, each targeting specific aspects of the connectivity problems.

The first hotfix on April 23 at 14:00 UTC addressed login queue overflow, reducing average wait times from 120 seconds to 45 seconds. A second patch deployed 8 hours later optimized matchmaking algorithms, decreasing session formation time from 4-5 minutes to 2-3 minutes. The most recent hotfix, released April 24 at 06:00 UTC, focused on session persistence, reducing mid-game disconnections by approximately 40%.
