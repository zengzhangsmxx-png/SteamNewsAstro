## BLAST Premier Expands Prize Pool and Format for 2026 Season

BLAST Premier has announced significant changes to its Counter-Strike 2 tournament structure for 2026, increasing the Spring Final prize pool to $1 million and expanding the field to 16 teams. The tournament, scheduled for June 12-16 at London's Copper Box Arena, represents a 25% increase from the previous year's $800,000 prize pool. This expansion comes as viewership for CS2 tournaments has grown by 18% compared to Counter-Strike: Global Offensive events in 2025, according to Esports Charts data.

The new format allocates 8 qualification spots to European teams, 4 to North American organizations, and 2 each to Asia-Pacific and South American regions. This distribution reflects current competitive strength while providing opportunities for emerging markets to showcase talent on the global stage.

## Tournament Structure and Qualification Changes

### Regional League Performance Requirements

Teams must maintain specific performance thresholds in their regional leagues to secure direct invitations to Premier events. The qualification criteria now includes:

| Region | Direct Slots | Minimum League Placement | Additional Requirements |

ESL Pro League has simultaneously announced changes to its qualification system, introducing open qualifiers for each region starting in Season 19. Previously, teams relied primarily on invitations based on world rankings and previous performance. The new system reserves 4 spots per region for open qualification, allowing unsigned rosters and lower-tier organizations to compete for Premier status.

## Valve's Official Tournament Guidelines Update

Valve Corporation released updated competitive guidelines for CS2 tournaments in March 2026, standardizing several format elements across major events. The MR12 format (first team to win 13 rounds) is now mandatory for all Valve-sponsored tournaments, replacing the traditional MR15 system used in Global Offensive.

The updated map pool rotation occurs every six months instead of annually, with Valve citing the need for "more dynamic competitive environments" in their official announcement. Current active duty maps include Dust2, Mirage, Inferno, Cache, Overpass, Vertigo, and Ancient, with Nuke temporarily removed for balance adjustments.

Tournament organizers must now provide minimum prize pools of $250,000 for events seeking Valve's Major status designation. This requirement has led to consolidation among smaller tournament organizers while encouraging larger prize pools from established brands like BLAST, ESL, and PGL.
