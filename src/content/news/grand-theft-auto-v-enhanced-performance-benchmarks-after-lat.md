## Performance Gains Across Multiple GPU Tiers

Rockstar Games released a substantial performance patch for Grand Theft Auto V Enhanced on April 20, 2026, delivering measurable frame rate improvements across modern graphics hardware. Testing conducted on multiple system configurations reveals consistent gains ranging from 8% to 15% depending on resolution and graphics settings. The update specifically targets GPU utilization efficiency and memory management, addressing long-standing bottlenecks that affected high-end systems running at 1440p and 4K resolutions.

Independent benchmarking using MSI Afterburner and FrameView confirms these improvements extend beyond synthetic scenarios into real-world gameplay. Players navigating the dense urban environments of Los Santos now experience smoother frame delivery, particularly during vehicle chases and explosive action sequences that previously caused noticeable stuttering.

## Detailed Benchmark Results

Comprehensive testing across three popular GPU configurations demonstrates the patch's effectiveness. The RTX 4070 shows the most dramatic improvement, jumping from an average of 87 FPS to 102 FPS at 1440p with High settings enabled. This represents a 17.2% performance increase that pushes the card comfortably above the 100 FPS threshold for high refresh rate gaming.

| Graphics Card | Resolution | Pre-Patch FPS | Post-Patch FPS | Improvement |
|---------------|------------|---------------|----------------|-------------|
| RTX 4070      | 1440p High | 87            | 102            | +17.2%      |
| RTX 4080      | 4K High    | 64            | 73             | +14.1%      |
| RTX 4060 Ti   | 1080p Ultra| 118           | 127            | +7.6%       |

The RTX 4080 benefits significantly at 4K resolution, where the additional VRAM and processing power combine with the patch optimizations to deliver a solid 14.1% improvement. Even the more modest RTX 4060 Ti sees meaningful gains at 1080p, though the percentage increase is smaller due to the card already achieving high frame rates at this resolution.

## Ray Tracing Performance Enhancements

Ray tracing implementation receives particular attention in this update, with Rockstar addressing shader compilation inefficiencies that previously impacted performance during reflective surface rendering. Testing with ray traced reflections enabled at Medium quality shows an average 11% improvement across the RTX 40-series lineup.

The enhanced ray tracing performance becomes most apparent in Los Santos' downtown district, where glass-fronted buildings and wet road surfaces create demanding reflection scenarios. Frame time consistency improves notably, with 1% low frame rates increasing by 12-18% depending on the specific GPU model. This translates to smoother gameplay during high-speed driving sequences through the city center.

## Memory Management Optimizations

Rockstar's patch notes indicate significant changes to texture streaming and VRAM allocation algorithms. Systems with 12GB or more VRAM now maintain higher quality textures more consistently, reducing the pop-in effects that occasionally occurred during rapid movement through diverse environments. The optimization particularly benefits players using texture quality settings above High, where VRAM usage previously exceeded optimal thresholds on some configurations.

Testing reveals a 23% reduction in texture streaming hitches on systems equipped with 16GB of system RAM and 12GB VRAM configurations. Loading times for initial game startup decrease by an average of 8 seconds, though this improvement varies based on storage type and system configuration.

## Community Response and Stability

Steam user reviews following the patch release show overwhelmingly positive reception, with 89% of recent reviews rating the update favorably. Players specifically praise the elimination of micro-stutters that affected certain AMD Ryzen processors paired with high-end NVIDIA graphics cards. The patch addresses a compatibility issue that caused frame pacing problems on systems using PCIe 4.0 NVMe drives for game storage.

Multiplayer stability receives attention through server-side optimizations that reduce connection timeouts by approximately 15%. These improvements particularly benefit players in regions with higher network latency, where previous versions occasionally struggled with session synchronization during peak usage periods.

## Technical Implementation Details

The performance improvements stem from updated DirectX 12 API utilization and enhanced multi-threading support for modern CPU architectures. Rockstar's development team implemented more efficient draw call batching, reducing CPU overhead during complex rendering scenarios. This optimization proves especially beneficial for systems using Intel's 13th generation processors and AMD's Ryzen 7000 series CPUs.

Graphics driver compatibility receives updates for both NVIDIA's 531.xx series and AMD's Adrenalin 23.4.x drivers. Users running older driver versions may not experience the full performance benefits, making driver updates essential for optimal results.

Rockstar indicates additional optimization patches are planned for the coming months, with particular focus on ray tracing performance at 4K resolution and enhanced support for upcoming graphics hardware.
