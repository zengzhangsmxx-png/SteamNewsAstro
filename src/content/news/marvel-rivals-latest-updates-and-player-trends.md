## Hero Rotation System Transforms Free-to-Play Experience

Marvel Rivals has implemented a comprehensive hero rotation system that fundamentally changes how players access the game's roster of 33 characters. Starting April 23, 2026, NetEase Games introduced weekly rotations featuring 12 heroes available to all players, regardless of ownership status. This update directly addresses community feedback about character accessibility, with 78% of surveyed players expressing concerns about the previous unlock system according to the official developer blog post published April 18, 2026.

The rotation refreshes every Tuesday at 12:00 UTC, ensuring consistent timing across all regions. Players retain access to any heroes they've purchased permanently, while the rotation provides opportunities to test new characters before committing to purchases. NetEase reported that this system increased average session length by 23 minutes during the first week of implementation.

## Ranked Mode Overhaul Introduces Competitive Structure

The ranked matchmaking system received substantial changes with the introduction of seven distinct tiers: Bronze, Silver, Gold, Platinum, Diamond, Master, and Grandmaster. Each tier contains five divisions, creating a total of 35 rank levels compared to the previous 20-level system. Players must complete 10 placement matches to receive their initial ranking, with seasonal resets occurring every three months.

Performance-based ranking adjustments now factor in individual statistics beyond win/loss ratios. The algorithm considers damage dealt, healing provided, objective time, and kill/death ratios when calculating rank progression. According to NetEase's April 20 patch notes, these metrics prevent support players from experiencing slower rank advancement compared to damage dealers.

## Balance Adjustments Target Competitive Meta

NetEase implemented targeted balance changes affecting eight heroes based on competitive play data from the previous season. Spider-Man's web-slinging cooldown increased from 6 to 8 seconds, while his wall-crawl speed decreased by 12% to address mobility advantages in ranked matches. Iron Man received damage buffs to his repulsor array, increasing base damage from 65 to 72 per shot.

Support heroes gained significant improvements, with Doctor Strange's healing rate increasing by 18% and his shield duration extending from 4 to 5.5 seconds. These changes directly respond to data showing support players comprised only 16% of ranked matches compared to the intended 25% distribution across roles.
