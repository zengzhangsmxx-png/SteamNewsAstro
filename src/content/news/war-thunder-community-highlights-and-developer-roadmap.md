## Naval Legends Program Brings Historic Warships to War Thunder

Gaijin Entertainment announced the Naval Legends program during their April developer livestream, introducing 12 meticulously recreated historic warships to War Thunder's naval combat system. The program launches with iconic vessels including USS Missouri, HMS Hood, Yamato, and Bismarck, each featuring detailed interior modeling and authentic crew communications recorded in their respective languages. According to the developer roadmap, Naval Legends enters closed beta testing in June 2026, with full integration planned for the Q3 2026 major update.

The announcement addresses long-standing community requests for more diverse naval content, as War Thunder's naval mode currently maintains approximately 15% of the game's active player base according to Steam achievement statistics. Community manager Anton Yudintsev confirmed that Naval Legends development began following feedback analysis from over 50,000 player surveys conducted throughout 2025.

## Ground Forces Physics Overhaul Details

Alongside Naval Legends, Gaijin revealed comprehensive improvements to ground vehicle physics affecting all 2,000+ tanks, armored vehicles, and self-propelled artillery in the game. The overhaul introduces enhanced suspension modeling, revised armor penetration calculations, and updated damage systems based on real-world ballistics data.

Key improvements include:

- Realistic suspension behavior for all vehicle types
- Updated armor effectiveness calculations using declassified military specifications
- Enhanced spalling and fragmentation effects
- Improved track and wheel physics on various terrain types

The physics update draws from collaboration with military museums and technical consultants, ensuring accuracy improvements without disrupting the game's established balance framework.

## Technical Specifications and Performance

Naval Legends ships feature unprecedented detail levels, with each vessel containing over 500 interactive components and authentic damage modeling. The USS Missouri model alone includes 1,200 individual parts, from functional turret mechanisms to accurate bridge instrumentation.

Performance optimization ensures Naval Legends maintains War Thunder's target of 60 FPS on recommended system specifications. Gaijin's technical team implemented level-of-detail scaling and dynamic loading systems to accommodate the increased polygon counts without compromising gameplay performance.

Ground forces physics improvements utilize updated collision detection algorithms, reducing CPU overhead by approximately 15% while delivering more realistic vehicle behavior. The enhanced damage modeling system processes ballistics calculations in real-time, supporting the game's commitment to authentic combat simulation.

## Release Timeline and Beta Access

The Naval Legends closed beta begins June 15, 2026, with access granted to players holding premium accounts and those selected through the community application process. Beta testing focuses on balancing, performance optimization, and multiplayer integration across War Thunder's existing game modes.

Ground forces physics improvements undergo parallel testing through the game's existing test server infrastructure. Gaijin plans staged rollouts beginning with select vehicle types before expanding to the complete ground forces roster.

Full release coordination targets September 2026, coinciding with War Thunder's traditional major update schedule. The development team emphasized that community feedback during beta testing will influence final implementation details and potential feature adjustments.

## Economic and Progression Impact

Both Naval Legends and ground forces improvements integrate with War Thunder's existing research and progression systems without requiring player account resets or vehicle re-grinding. Naval Legends ships occupy new research lines within existing naval trees, while ground forces improvements apply automatically to owned vehicles.

Gaijin confirmed that Silver Lions and Research Points requirements remain unchanged, addressing community concerns about progression disruption. Premium vehicle owners receive automatic access to physics improvements, maintaining their investment value within the updated systems.

The developer roadmap indicates continued focus on community-requested features throughout 2026, with quarterly updates planned to address ongoing player feedback and technical refinements. War Thunder's commitment to authentic military simulation continues driving development priorities, balancing historical accuracy with engaging gameplay mechanics.
